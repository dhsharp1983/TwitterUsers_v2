App intro and reason for analysis

Twitter - it's the leading microblogging platform worldwide. Users from all walks of life, including celebrities, politicians, news media outlets, sports clubs, and of course, everyday people, post more than 750 million 'tweets' per day, taking part in a vast social network where users peruse, like, comment on, and share other users' tweets.

Due to Twitter's huge user base of almost 200 million users, as well as all those who peruse Twitter accounts without actually having an account, Twitter, like its big social network counterparts Facebook and Instagram, is a major vehicle for advertisement. Not only of the commercial variety, but also for opinions, meaning Twitter's omnipresence might just have an influence on how people think. The top 50 Twitter users, with 30 to 130 million followers each, especially have the potential to influence.

Have you liked some of their most liked tweets? Have you found yourself feeling positive or negative after reading one of their tweets? Or are you simply interested in what famous people talk about and to whom? Then our data might be interesting to you.
